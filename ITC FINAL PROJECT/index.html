Project Showcase

Cassava-Starch Based Bioplastic Mulch Films Reinforced with Saba Banana Pseudostem Fibers

This project investigates the development of biodegradable bioplastic mulch films as an eco-friendly alternative to conventional plastic mulching materials in agriculture. These bioplastics are composed of cassava starch as the base material and reinforced with fibers extracted from Saba banana pseudostems. The research aligns with United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production) by addressing issues such as plastic pollution and soil health degradation.

Features and Highlights:

  • Biodegradability: The bioplastic films decompose naturally without leaving harmful residues, reducing long-term environmental impact.
  • Moisture Retention: Enhances soil moisture conservation for improved plant growth.
  • Temperature Regulation: Maintains optimal soil temperatures suitable for crop production.
  • Reinforcement with Natural Fibers: Integrates banana pseudostem fibers to improve tensile strength and reduce combustibility.

Benefits:

  • Offers a sustainable alternative to traditional polyethylene mulch.
  • Reduces dependency on synthetic chemicals for agriculture.
  • Enhances soil quality while mitigating waste generation from single-use plastics.

SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production

This section delves into Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 12, which focuses on ensuring sustainable consumption and production patterns. The four key topics outlined below explore different ways in which industries, communities, and individuals can contribute to this global goal:

Sustainable Production

  • • Focuses on minimizing environmental impacts and conserving natural resources.
  • • Adopt energy-efficient practices, eco-friendly materials, and sustainable manufacturing processes.

Responsible Consumption

  • • Encourages mindful consumption, reducing waste, and opting for environmentally friendly products.
  • • Promotes awareness of resource depletion and waste generation.

Waste Reduction

  • • Focuses on minimizing waste generation and enhancing recycling efforts.
  • • Promotes a circular economy model that reuses and repurposes materials.

Responsible Resource Management

  • • Ensures efficient use of resources, prioritizing sustainability over exploitation.
  • • Supports fair trade practices and the responsible sourcing of raw materials.
